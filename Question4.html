Question 4: Explanation of your novel's biographical and historical context based on your research on:

There are many aspects of Brandon Sanderson's life, and world events near the time of the publishing of this book that could have had an inspiration to elements of The Way Of Kings. Brandon Sanderson grew up in and continued to be a part of the LDS church (latter day saints). Some elements of religious morality and divinity can often be found in all of Sanderson's literature, but especially with the character dalinar in the way of kings. In this book we have consistent mentions of an unnamed deity referred to as “the stormfather.” a revered and divine figure, lining up with some Mormon theology in the reverence of God.
Sanderson's life included its own struggles, having written 13 unpublished novels before having the novel “Elantris” picked up by TOR books publishing. Brandon's relation to being unsuccessful in his goals (originally) and persevering can potentially be related to the perseverance of characters like Dalinar and Kaladin. Dalinar when all troops do nothing but point out his flaws and more specifically his growing madness, having to endure the doubt of those supposedly loyal to him. And Kaladin through many physical trials and depression and self doubt/worth.
The original manuscript of this book was written in 2002. In a shortly post 9/11 world. Sanderson writes a book that has neither a “good guy” or a “bad guy.” Yes, there are protagonists and antagonists. But there are no moral good or bad people in this book. This would have extremely contrasted the mass media in this time. Where after tragic events lead to the need for a good versus evil conflict in the world. When the reality is, war is never about good versus evil, but all selfish gain and retaliation. These are some ways that events in or during Brandon sandersons life before the way of Kings

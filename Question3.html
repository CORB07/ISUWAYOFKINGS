Question 3: Character developement

What have you learned about the main characters from the first third of the novel?

Kaladin

Kaladin is introduced to the reader as a brave Alethi warrior, Fighting for Brightlord Amaram, the Brightlord of his region, in a petty border dispute against a neighbouring Brightlord. This dispute ended quickly for Amaram's army due to his enemy possessing a full Shardbearer.
Kaladin was a highly respected squadron leader in Brightlord Amaram's army, and was given the title "Stormblessed" by his squad. Kaladin, in the swift defeat, is sold as a slave. This is where Kaladin's story truly begins, as he is brought to be a Bridgeman in Brightlord Sadeas's army in the War against the Parshendi.
Kaladin loses hope within the first segment of this novel. Crushed by the misfortune brought to him, and all the people he's watched die. But shortly after his arrival to Bridge Four, Kaladin finds where he is needed and assumes the role of Bridgeleader. The rest of Bridge Four are too broken to care and ignore him.

Shallan

Shallan is introduced as a girl who desires to become Jasnah Kholins ward. Claiming to have a desire to study under her, but secretly actually wanting to steal her soulcaster. Shallans father had a soulcaster, but it broke shortly after his death. Leaving Shallans' family with no source of income, soon to be swallowed by the other Alethi Noble families. Shallan spends this whole section of the book trying to convince Jasnah to take her in. By the end of this section, Jasnah agrees. Shallan is a desperate character, willing to do anything she can to keep her family's reputation alive.

Dalinar

Dalinar is introduced as a HighPrince and Brightlord in the Alethi army. His brother was King Gavilar, who was murdered 7 years prior to the events of most of this book. In the past 7 years the Alethi armies have been against the Parshendi people. Who claimed responsibility for the assasination of Gavilar. Dalinar clings to the ghost of his brother. Trying desperately to not allow the kingdom to lose sight of the values brought by the past king. Rumours spread of Dalinar losing himself to madness. Having fits during highstorms. Dalinar feels as if his brother's death is his own fault, as he was in a drunken sleep during the assasination. And now feels as if it is his job alone to keep king Elothkar (Gavilarâ€™s son) alive.
Dalinar is a character with everything stacked up against him. He is losing his way, and stubbornly refusing to accept a changing world. Dalinar is a character who has profound wisdom, yet appears to everyone else as a fool.

Adolin

Adolin is the son of Dalinar, who has to watch as his father starts to lose support of his fathers own armies. Adolin is young, and does not yet have the wisdom of his father. He is quick tempered and tends to duel anyone who talks poorly of his father. His main weakness, as pointed out by his father, is his raw hatred and distrust of Brightlord Sadeas. Adolin is a character who will need to mature in some areas, before being able to take over his fathers armies.

How have the main characters changed, grown, or revealed themselves in the second third of the novel?

Kaladin

As Kaladins story continues, some of the other members of bridge four begin to see kaladin as a leader. Kal begins to care greatly for the people around, giving all he has to tend to his wounded, spending hours and hours finding medicine and bandages. Kal starts to feel like he has once again found purpose. Kal grows greatly as a character in this section of the book. Kal also makes some allies in this section, where previously he talked to no one for fear of them dying.

Shallan

Shallan is not a character in this section of the book.

Dalinar

Dalinars visions worsen in this second third of the book. More frequently he is haunted by his brother's words. Dalinar begins to dress more traditionally, and have ancient texts of the Knights Radiant read to him as he retires at a day's end. More and more rumours spread as some soldiers are present during one of his collapses. More and more people begin to believe he's gone mad. Dalinar begins to think his visions are from the Almighty. Dalinar grows bolder in this section, but people's trust in him regresses. Dalinar tries to create peace treaties with the other Alethi BrightLords, but all others refuse, as the Alethi ways of war are meant to be of sport and competition. At the end of this section. Dalinar declares his intention to retire and hand his armies to his son Adolin.

Adolin

In this section Adolin comes to accept greater responsibility, knowing that his time will soon come where his fathers armies will be his own. He takes greater care for the details of protecting the king, especially as a suspicious incident related to the king's saddle takes place. He learns to slow his anger towards Sadeas, and to pretend to trust him in the way that his father pretends. Adolin grows greatly in this section, trying to become the man his father once was.
